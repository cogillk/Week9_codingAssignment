Book Title Author Rating Review
Extremely Loud & Incredibly Close Jonathan Safran Foer 9/10 The book is written from a Oskar Schell's point of view, a nine-year-old in search of the meaning behind a key of his father's that he found a year after losing him to the September 11 attach. Oskar ventures out of the streets of New York and is meets all sorts of characters. This novel made me laugh, it made me cry. It's a book that I wish I could read again for the first time. It is so well written that I was Oskar myself, wondering the city streets in search of the meaning behind the mysterious key and at the same time, closure.
The Goldfinch Donna Tart 8/10 This novel, while it was a long read, was an incredible read. Its part coming-of-age and part mystery. Theo Decker faces a great deal of loss and pain, but ends up finding closure, and more importantly, hope. This book is about more than that, though. It reaches into many corners of life and takes you along for the ride. This novel is equally as epic as it is heartbreaking, it's simply mesmerizing.
The Weight Of Ink Rachel Kadish 9/10 The Weight of Ink is a novel that touches on philosophy and history as it takes you on a journey from the 1660's to the twenty-first century in London. This tale is an interwoven story of two remarkable women, Ester Velasquez, an emigrant from Amsterdam who is a scribe for a blind rabbi, just before the plague hits the city; and Helen Watt, an ailing historian with a love of Jewish history. The story of these women dive into human nature, revealing the human thoughts, feelings and it takes you through the of understanding one's inner nature. This novel was incredibly well written, the character development speaks for itself. The relationships between the people in this book are so well written that I felt I was evolving along with them. A novel that I will be sure to read again.
